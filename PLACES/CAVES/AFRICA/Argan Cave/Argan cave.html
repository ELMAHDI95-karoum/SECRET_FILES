ARGAN CAVE

Introduction

Deep within the Souss mountains of Morocco, near the village of Idauknif, lies the desolate Argan Cave, a place shunned for decades. Local lore claims that anyone who speaks inside will hear their echo, but it never returns to them.

Theories

In 1912, a researcher named Said entered the cave, defying the elders' warnings, only to find unnervingly smooth walls and a voice that repeated his words after a strange delay. Said emerged pale and silent, leaving behind a note that read: "The echo does not return from there... it continues the conversation with those inside." To this day, it is believed the cave is not merely a place, but a portal for voices that lost their way back, where passersby hear their names called from the darkness, yet no echo ever follows.
