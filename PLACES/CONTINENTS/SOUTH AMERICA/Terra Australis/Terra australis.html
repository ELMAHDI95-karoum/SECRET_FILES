TERRA AUSTRALIS

Introduction

Research

Before Antarctica was officially discovered, European mapmakers imagined a vast southern continent — Terra Australis Incognita — a "hidden land of the south" that balanced the known world. In 1531, French cartographer Orontius Finaeus published a world map that boldly depicted this mythical continent in striking detail.

Finaeus’ map shows a massive southern landmass with river systems, mountain ranges, and even ports — long before any confirmed exploration of Antarctic regions. Some later speculated whether this was based on ancient knowledge, lost voyages, or pure speculation.
